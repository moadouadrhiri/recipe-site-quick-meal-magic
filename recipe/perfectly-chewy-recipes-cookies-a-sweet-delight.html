# Ingredients You'll Need:
01 - 1 cup flour, all-purpose, spooned and leveled
02 - 1/2 cup brown sugar, packed
03 - 1/4 cup granulated sugar
04 - 1/2 cup butter, softened
05 - 1 egg, large
06 - 1 tsp vanilla extract
07 - 1/2 tsp baking soda
08 - 1/4 tsp salt
# How to Make It:
01 - Preheat your oven to 350F (175C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper. This ensures even baking and prevents sticking.
02 - In a large mixing bowl, cream together the butter, brown sugar, and granulated sugar until light and fluffy. This incorporates air, giving the cookies a light texture.
03 - Add the egg and vanilla extract to the sugar mixture, beating until fully combined. This step ensures a uniform dough consistency.
04 - In another bowl, whisk together the flour, baking soda, and salt. Gradually add this dry mixture to the wet ingredients, mixing just until combined.
05 - Chill the dough in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes. This step helps the flavors develop and prevents spreading during baking.
06 - Scoop tablespoon-sized portions of dough onto the prepared baking sheet, spacing them about 2 inches apart. This allows room for spreading.
07 - Bake in the preheated oven for 10-12 minutes, or until the edges are lightly golden. The centers should still appear slightly underbaked for chewy cookies.
08 - Allow the cookies to cool on the baking sheet for 5 minutes before transferring to a wire rack to cool completely. This helps them firm up without overcooking.
# Extra Information:
01 - Ensure butter is softened to room temperature for ease of mixing.
02 - Chilling the dough prevents excessive spreading and enhances flavor.
03 - Rotate baking sheets halfway through for even baking.
04 - Store cookies in airtight containers to maintain freshness.
05 - Experiment with different add-ins like nuts or chocolate chips for variety.
06 - Add 1 cup of chocolate chips to the dough for a classic twist.
07 - Replace half the flour with oats for a hearty texture.
08 - Proper storage ensures your recipes cookies retain their delightful texture and flavor. At room temperature, store the cookies in an airtight container for up to 5 days. This prevents them from drying out and keeps them fresh. For longer storage, refrigerate the cookies in a sealed container for up to 10 days. Refrigeration can sometimes affect texture, so allow them to come to room temperature before serving. Freezing is an excellent optionarrange cookies in a single layer in a freezer-safe bag, separating layers with parchment paper, and freeze for up to 3 months. When reheating, the oven is your best bet to restore the cookies' original texture. Bake at 300F (150C) for 5 minutes or until just warmed through. For a quicker method, a short microwave burst of 10-15 seconds can work, though it may soften the cookies more than desired.

Perfectly Chewy Recipes Cookies - A Sweet Delight

Print Recipe
Pin Recipe

Indulge in these perfectly chewy recipes cookies, a sweet delight that's easy to make and impossible to resist. Discover the magic today!

  • Total Time: 32 minutes
  • Yield: 24 servings

Ingredients

  • flour: 1 cup - all-purpose, spooned and leveled
  • brown sugar: 1/2 cup - packed
  • granulated sugar: 1/4 cup
  • butter: 1/2 cup - softened
  • egg: 1 - large
  • vanilla extract: 1 tsp
  • baking soda: 1/2 tsp
  • salt: 1/4 tsp

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350F (175C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper. This ensures even baking and prevents sticking.

    Tip: Always preheat your oven for at least 15 minutes for accurate temperature.

  2. In a large mixing bowl, cream together the butter, brown sugar, and granulated sugar until light and fluffy. This incorporates air, giving the cookies a light texture.

    Tip: Use room temperature butter for easier mixing and better texture.

  3. Add the egg and vanilla extract to the sugar mixture, beating until fully combined. This step ensures a uniform dough consistency.

    Tip: Crack the egg in a separate bowl to avoid shell pieces.

  4. In another bowl, whisk together the flour, baking soda, and salt. Gradually add this dry mixture to the wet ingredients, mixing just until combined.

    Tip: Over-mixing can lead to tough cookies, so mix gently.

  5. Chill the dough in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes. This step helps the flavors develop and prevents spreading during baking.

    Tip: Cover the bowl with plastic wrap to prevent the dough from drying out.

  6. Scoop tablespoon-sized portions of dough onto the prepared baking sheet, spacing them about 2 inches apart. This allows room for spreading.

    Tip: Use a cookie scoop for evenly sized cookies.

  7. Bake in the preheated oven for 10-12 minutes, or until the edges are lightly golden. The centers should still appear slightly underbaked for chewy cookies.

    Tip: Rotate the baking sheet halfway through for even baking.

  8. Allow the cookies to cool on the baking sheet for 5 minutes before transferring to a wire rack to cool completely. This helps them firm up without overcooking.

    Tip: Cooling on the sheet ensures chewy centers.

Notes

  • Ensure butter is softened to room temperature for ease of mixing.
  • Chilling the dough prevents excessive spreading and enhances flavor.
  • Rotate baking sheets halfway through for even baking.
  • Store cookies in airtight containers to maintain freshness.
  • Experiment with different add-ins like nuts or chocolate chips for variety.

Frequently Asked Questions for Recipes cookies

What makes these recipes cookies chewy?
The combination of brown sugar, room temperature butter, and precise baking time ensures a chewy texture.

Can I substitute butter in recipes cookies?
Yes, margarine or coconut oil can be used, though flavors and texture may vary slightly.

How do I prevent cookies from spreading too much?
Chill the dough before baking and use parchment paper to help control spreading.

Can I freeze cookie dough for later use?
Absolutely! Freeze dough balls on a tray, then transfer to a freezer bag for up to 3 months.

Why is my cookie dough too sticky?
This may result from overly soft butter or warm kitchen conditions. Chill the dough to firm it up.

How can I make recipes cookies gluten-free?
Use a 1:1 gluten-free flour blend to replace all-purpose flour without compromising texture.

What's the best way to measure flour?
Spoon flour into your measuring cup, then level it with a knife for accuracy.
